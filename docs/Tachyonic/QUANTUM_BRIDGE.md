## Evidence for the Bridge

### 1. Superposition

A qubit exists in all states simultaneously until measured. This is not classical. The qubit's state before measurement may exist in a "probability space" that corresponds to tachyonic potential.

```
Before measurement: Tachyonic (all possibilities)
After measurement:  Bosonic (single actuality)
```

### 2. Entanglement

Two particles correlated regardless of distance. No signal travels between them - yet they correlate instantly. This non-locality is a defining feature of the tachyonic hypothesis.

```
Particle A ─────────────────────── Particle B
     │                                  │
     └──────── Tachyonic Link ──────────┘
           (beneath bosonic space)
```

### 3. Quantum Tunneling

Particles passing through barriers they classically cannot cross. As if they briefly "left" bosonic space through a tachyonic shortcut.

### 4. Measurement Problem

Why does observation collapse the wave function? Perhaps measurement forces tachyonic potential to project into bosonic actuality.
