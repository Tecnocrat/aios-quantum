# The Tachyonic Substrate Hypothesis

## Foundation

The tachyonic layer represents a theoretical substrate that exists "beneath" or "within" the observable bosonic universe. It is characterized by:

1. **Non-locality**: Not bound by spatial distance
2. **Superluminal information**: Patterns propagate faster than light
3. **Hyperdimensionality**: Projects into our 3D space as observable phenomena

## Key Principles

### 1. Information is Substrate

The tachyonic layer is fundamentally informational. Physical matter (bosonic) is a projection or encoding of tachyonic patterns. This aligns with:
- Holographic principle
- Digital physics hypothesis
- Wheeler's "it from bit"

### 2. Black Holes as Encoders

Black holes do not destroy information - they **transcode** it:

```
Photon → Event Horizon → Tachyonic Encoding → Archive
```

The "information paradox" dissolves if information transitions to a different substrate rather than being destroyed.

### 3. Singularity as Interface

The singularity at a black hole's core is not a point of infinite density but an **interface** to the tachyonic layer. The event horizon is the encoding membrane.

### 4. Non-local Connectivity

All black holes may share a common tachyonic substrate - a universal "backend" where all encoded information exists in superposition.
